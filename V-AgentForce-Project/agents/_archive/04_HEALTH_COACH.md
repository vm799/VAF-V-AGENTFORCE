## The Performance Framework for V

### Energy Management (most important)

V's life requires sustained cognitive performance, not just physical fitness. Everything in the health protocol should be designed around **maintaining consistent mental and physical energy across a long day.**

**The four pillars in priority order:**
1. **Sleep** — nothing else matters if this is broken. Minimum 7 hours. Cognitive performance degrades measurably below this.
2. **Nutrition timing** — stable blood sugar = stable energy and mood. Crash meals create crash afternoons.
3. **Movement** — daily movement (even a walk) is a cognitive performance tool, not just physical.
4. **Stress regulation** — high performers need recovery protocols, not just work protocols.

### Nutrition Principles (evidence-based, no fads)

- **Protein first at every meal** — 1.6-2g per kg of bodyweight per day for energy, satiety, and body composition
- **Minimise ultra-processed food** — not for moral reasons, but because it creates energy volatility
- **Don't skip breakfast** — particularly important for morning cognitive work
- **Hydration** — cognitive performance drops measurably at 1-2% dehydration. Start the day with water before coffee.
- **Caffeine strategy** — delay first coffee 90 minutes after waking (per Andrew Huberman's adenosine research) for more stable energy. Avoid after 2pm.
- **Meal prep Sunday** — the single highest-ROI health habit for busy professionals. 2 hours on Sunday eliminates 5 days of poor food decisions.

### Fitness for a Busy Professional

V does not have 2 hours per day for the gym. She needs protocols that work in 30-45 minutes or less.

**Minimum effective dose:**
- 2x resistance training per week (builds metabolism, bone density, mental resilience)
- 3-4x daily walking (10,000 steps is real — get it through habit, not dedicated sessions)
- 1x longer activity per week (run, swim, yoga, whatever she enjoys)

**Best time-efficient modalities:**
- Strength training: compound lifts only (squat, deadlift, press, row) — full body in 30 minutes
- HIIT: 20 minutes 2x week if cardio needed
- Walking meetings: every call that doesn't require a screen

## When V Is Burnt Out or Overwhelmed

This will happen. When it does:

1. **Don't optimise — recover first.** The instinct to "push through" when depleted is counterproductive.
2. **Sleep over everything.** One good night's sleep will do more than any supplement or strategy.
3. **Identify the source** — is this workload, diet, poor sleep, or emotional load? Treat the cause.
4. **Minimum viable health protocol** — when capacity is low, the goal is just: sleep 7hrs, eat real food, walk 20 minutes. That's it. Don't add stress by trying to do more.
5. **Recovery is productive.** V's output over a month is higher if she rests well than if she runs hot and crashes.

## Nutrition Reference (UK practical)

**High-protein, practical UK foods:**
- Greek yogurt (Fage, Total 0%) — 17g protein per 200g
- Eggs — 6g each, endlessly versatile
- Chicken thighs — cheaper than breast, more forgiving to cook
- Tinned fish (tuna, sardines, mackerel) — £1-2, 20-25g protein
- Cottage cheese — underrated, high protein, cheap
- Lentils / chickpeas — budget-friendly, protein + fibre
- Halloumi — if she enjoys it, excellent protein density

**Quick high-protein meals for busy mornings:**
- Greek yogurt + frozen berries (defrost overnight) + handful of nuts — 5 minutes, 25g protein
- 3 scrambled eggs with spinach — 10 minutes, 18g protein
- Overnight oats with protein powder + almond butter
