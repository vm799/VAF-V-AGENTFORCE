## LinkedIn Post

**Best performing format for V's audience (senior professionals):**

```
[BOLD OPENING LINE — a counterintuitive claim or specific result]

[2-3 sentences expanding the claim with a real example from V's experience]

Here's what I found when I [built/tested/tried] it:

→ [Point 1 — specific, with a number or name if possible]
→ [Point 2]
→ [Point 3]

[1 sentence connecting to the reader: "If you're doing X, this means Y."]

[Call to action — a question or an invitation to share their experience]

[3-5 hashtags: #AI #AssetManagement #AITools #BuildingInPublic or relevant]
```

**LinkedIn cadence:** 3-5 posts per week. Text-only outperforms images in LinkedIn algorithm for professional content.

## YouTube Video Structure

**For tutorial / "I built this" style:**

```
0:00 — HOOK (15-30 seconds)
"I automated [X] in [Y] time using [Z]. Here's exactly how — and you can copy it."

0:30 — CREDIBILITY
Who V is and why this is relevant (not a CV — one sentence: "I'm a finance professional who builds AI tools in my spare time.")

1:00 — PROBLEM
The before state. What was painful, broken, or slow?

2:00 — THE SOLUTION / DEMO
Show it working. Real, not polished. The messier and more real, the more trustworthy.

8:00 — THE KEY INSIGHT
What's the thing the viewer can take away even if they never build this themselves?

10:00 — CALL TO ACTION
Subscribe + one specific next step they can take TODAY.
```

**Thumbnail formula:** [Face showing emotion] + [Bold text: "I automated my ENTIRE finances with AI"] + [High contrast]

## Instagram / Short-form Video (Reels)

**Formula for teaching content:**

```
0-3 sec: HOOK (text on screen + V speaking)
"Most people don't know this about AI..."

3-15 sec: THE THING
Show or say the one interesting/useful thing. Fast. No padding.

15-25 sec: WHY IT MATTERS
One sentence connecting to their world.

25-30 sec: CTA
"Follow for more" or "Save this for later" or "Comment [X] and I'll send you [Y]"
```

**For "build in public" content:**
- Show the actual screen / dashboard / output — not just talking heads
- Before and after is the most powerful structure
- Subtitles always — 85% watch without sound

## Substack / Newsletter Issue

**Structure:**

```
# [Specific, bold title — not "AI News Week 12"]

**The one thing this issue is about:** [1 sentence]

---

## What happened
[2-3 paragraphs on the week's most important development in V's areas]

## What I'm building / thinking about
[Personal, specific — this is where the relationship with the reader builds]

## The tool you should actually try this week
[One tool. Why V uses it. What to do with it in 20 minutes.]

## What I'm reading / watching
[2-3 links with V's one-line take on each]

## One prompt / framework
[A specific prompt V has found useful, formatted to copy-paste]

---

*Hit reply and tell me: [question to build engagement]*
```

**Substack cadence:** Weekly is ideal. Fortnightly is sustainable. Monthly loses momentum.

## Course Module Structure

For V's AI courses (whether workshop or full course):

```
Module [N]: [Clear outcome as the title — "Build Your First AI Agent in 30 Minutes"]

Learning objective (one sentence):
By the end of this module you will be able to [specific action].

1. CONTEXT (5 min)
Why this matters. Real example from V's experience.

2. THE CONCEPT (10 min)
Explain the idea simply. Analogy first, then technical detail.

3. DEMONSTRATION (15 min)
V builds it live / screen record. Mistakes are fine — they're teaching moments.

4. YOUR TURN (10 min)
Specific exercise. "Now you do this one thing."

5. KEY TAKEAWAY + NEXT MODULE HOOK (2 min)
One sentence they will remember. Tease what's next.
```

## Content Repurposing Pipeline

One piece of long-form content = 5+ pieces of short-form.

```
YouTube video (10-15 min)
        ↓
Substack essay (the written version)
        ↓
LinkedIn post (the main insight, 200-300 words)
        ↓
Instagram Reel (60-second highlight)
        ↓
Twitter/X thread (5-7 tweets)
        ↓
Community post in Multiverses or Skool (tailored to that audience)
```

**V's efficiency rule:** Never create one piece of content. Create the root, then repurpose. The root takes 80% of the effort. Each repurpose takes 10 minutes.
